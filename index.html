KineSAM: Active Kinematic Correction of SAM 2
for Zero-Shot Dynamic Eye-in-Hand Manipulation

Abstract

While the vanilla SAM 2 possesses powerful segmentation capabilities, it faces severe challenges in eye-in-hand manipulation due to the manipulator's ego-motion and frequent field-of-view occlusions. To address these issues, we propose KineSAM, a zero-shot framework that couples a Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM 2) based kinematic-aware semantic perceiver (KASP) with an active perception controller (APC). The APC leverages proprioceptive feedback to correct motion-induced deviations, while SAM 2 supplies zero-shot semantic perception. Furthermore, our dual-confidence memory selection strategy enforces visual-kinematic consensus to suppress distractor-induced identity (ID) switches. Experimental results demonstrate that KineSAM significantly outperforms baselines in both success rates and robustness.

Accelerated Policy Training via Lightweight Surrogate Model

Conclusion

In this work, we presented KineSAM, seamlessly connecting semantic understanding to physical manipulation in a closed-loop fashion. By leveraging a bidirectional cross-module information channel, KineSAM significantly suppresses distractor-induced ID switches and facilitates active target re-acquisition after prolonged occlusions. The introduction of the surrogate model drastically improves RL training efficiency. Extensive experiments demonstrate its robustness and zero-shot transferability in dynamic, cluttered real-world environments.
