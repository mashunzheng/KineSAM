KineSAM: Active Kinematic Correction of SAM 2
for Zero-Shot Dynamic Eye-in-Hand Manipulation

Paper Model / Core Snippets

Abstract

While the vanilla SAM 2 possesses powerful segmentation capabilities, it faces severe challenges in eye-in-hand manipulation due to the manipulator's ego-motion and frequent field-of-view occlusions. To address these issues, we propose KineSAM, a zero-shot framework that couples a Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM 2) based kinematic-aware semantic perceiver (KASP) with an active perception controller (APC). The APC leverages proprioceptive feedback to correct motion-induced deviations, while SAM 2 supplies zero-shot semantic perception. Furthermore, our dual-confidence memory selection strategy enforces visual-kinematic consensus to suppress distractor-induced identity (ID) switches. Experimental results demonstrate that KineSAM significantly outperforms baselines in both success rates and robustness.

Overview

System overview of KineSAM. The KASP module provides semantic observations, while the APC uses kinematic beliefs to regulate KASP, facilitating target recovery and preventing interference from distractors.

Efficient Training via Neural Perception Surrogate

Directly deploying SAM 2 in the RL training loop incurs prohibitive computational costs. To isolate foundation model inference from policy optimization, we developed a lightweight neural network-based surrogate model.

  • Models SAM 2's specific noise profiles, bounding box jitter, and ID-switch behaviors.
  • Reduces PPO training time (8M steps) from ~5 days to approximately 1.3 hours on a single NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPU.
  • In MuJoCo simulations, we rigorously equalized the mass and friction of objects and distractors in the MSD task to ensure strictly consistent physical properties for Multi-Similar Distractor validation.

Conclusion

In this work, we presented KineSAM, seamlessly connecting semantic understanding to physical manipulation in a closed-loop fashion. By leveraging a bidirectional cross-module information channel, KineSAM significantly suppresses distractor-induced ID switches and facilitates active target re-acquisition after prolonged occlusions. The introduction of the neural surrogate model drastically improves RL training efficiency. Extensive experiments demonstrate its robustness and zero-shot transferability in dynamic, cluttered real-world environments.
